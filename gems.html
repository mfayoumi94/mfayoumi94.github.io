TAMUK Engineering Challenge 2016

In this challenge students from our campus were taken to the Texas A&M University of Kingsville to compete with other students on an engineering challenge. Each year a specific topic is selected and in the most recent competition, wind power turned out to be the topic. Follow our team in their journey below to explore how they found the path to success.

Success

Students were given 15 minutes to design their solution and use their electronic devices to research information on the problem

The team created their design with the limited resources that were provided to them while keeping their constraints in mind.

After hard work and tedius construction, the team was ready for the final step: presenting their solution to the judges.

Applying the engineering principles the students learned at school, they created the most effective and efficient designs.




Girls Excelling in Engineering and Math

GEMS (Girls Excelling in Math and Science) has been working since 1994 to increase interest in STEM for girls in elementary and middle school and to expose girls to the fun and wonder of these fields. Our Campus has decided to take part in this organization and currently hosts various Saturday workshops where elementary aged girls are offered exposure to engineering, math, and science at no cost whatsoever.
